Comparison

For comparing air pollution levels and SIVIC rates, we shall focus on the middle graph at air pollution.

Upon looking closely, we find out that nitrogen oxides levels and ARI diagnosis throughout the year follow the same wave variation pattern:

  • They both begin high at their relative baseline.
  • They both decrease gradually until the arrival of autumn.
  • They're both at their lowest during august.
  • And they both raise back to exceeding their respective baselines from september until the end of the year.

Conclusion

Under this interpretation, our graphs indicate a clear direct proportion between Acute Respiratory Infections and Nitrogen Oxide in the air, suggesting that either one is a causant/contributor of the other (elevated NO* levels leading to ARIs) or that they're both common effects originated from a third variable that we haven't laid our eyes upon yet.
